About Me

Welcome!

I'm Johannes Kirmayr, a PhD student bridging academic research and industrial innovation. In collaboration with BMW Group, I'm pursuing an external doctorate at the University of Augsburg. My research explores how Large Language Models (LLMs) can enhance in-car voice interaction, with a focus on advanced planning and reasoning. I investigate techniques like fine-tuning and reinforcement learning to improve agentic behavior and conduct user studies to evaluate the experience of multi-step, long-horizon voice assistants.

2025

CarMem: Enhancing Long-Term Memory in LLM Voice Assistants through Category-Bounding

J. Kirmayr, L. Stappen, P. Schneider, F. Matthes, E. Andre

Proceedings of the 31st International Conference on Computational Linguistics: Industry Track (COLING 2025)

Abstract: In today's assistant landscape, personalisation enhances interactions, fosters long-term relationships, and deepens engagement. However, many systems struggle with retaining user preferences, leading to repetitive user requests and disengagement. Furthermore, the unregulated and opaque extraction of user preferences in industry applications raises significant concerns about privacy and trust, especially in regions with stringent regulations like Europe. In response to these challenges, we propose a long-term memory system for voice assistants, structured around predefined categories. This approach leverages Large Language Models to efficiently extract, store, and retrieve preferences within these categories, ensuring both personalisation and transparency. We also introduce a synthetic multi-turn, multi-session conversation dataset (CarMem), grounded in real industry data, tailored to an in-car voice assistant setting. Benchmarked on the dataset, our system achieves an F1-score of .78 to .95 in preference extraction, depending on category granularity. Our maintenance strategy reduces redundant preferences by 95% and contradictory ones by 92%, while the accuracy of optimal retrieval is at .87. Collectively, the results demonstrate the system's suitability for industrial applications.

2022

Learning the Koopman Eigendecomposition: A Diffeomorphic Approach

P. Bevanda, J. Kirmayr, S. Sosnowski, S. Hirche

2022 American Control Conference (ACC)

Abstract: This paper presents a novel diffeomorphic approach to learning Koopman eigendecomposition, providing a data-driven framework for understanding the spectral properties of dynamical systems. Our method leverages advanced mathematical techniques to decompose complex dynamical behaviors into simpler, interpretable components. The approach is particularly valuable for control applications where understanding the underlying system dynamics is crucial for effective control design. We demonstrate the effectiveness of our method on various benchmark problems and show its potential for real-world applications in robotics and autonomous systems.
